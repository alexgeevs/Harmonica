A music player that won't wear your songs out.

Harmonica is a local-first, self-hosted player for your own library. Instead of shuffle, its algorithm tracks how much play each song, artist and topic can still give you — and spends your listening where it still counts.

Download View source on GitHub

Why not just shuffle?

Everyone knows the failure mode: you find songs you love, you play them constantly, and a few weeks later they're ruined — no novelty left, no joy left, and the rest of your library sat untouched the whole time.

Harmonica treats that as the central problem. Every song belongs to weighted groups — its artist, its topics — and the algorithm keeps score of recent plays at the song, group and version level. Recently-hammered material cools down; dormant favourites warm back up; unheard songs get their first fair chance. The queue it builds isn't random: it's the set of songs most likely to actually be worth your next hour.

What it does

  • Cooldowns, not shuffle

    Weighted queues with per-song, per-artist and per-topic cooldowns, so no corner of your library gets over-played.

  • Satiation guard

    Notices a binge before you burn out, and paces the song so it recovers instead of souring.

  • Rediscovery

    Well-rated songs you haven't heard in months quietly regain weight and come back around.

  • Fair cold start

    New and unrated songs are boosted until everything in the library has had a proper hearing.

  • Ratings that stay honest

    Star ratings with mood normalisation — one grumpy evening doesn't permanently sink a good song.

  • Household-ready

    Per-user profiles over one shared media pool: private libraries, ratings and listening history for each person on the same NAS.

Setting it up: Harmonica is self-hosted, and honestly, doing the setup by hand is tedious. The intended path is to hand the repository to an AI coding agent and let it install, configure, and import your library for you. Agents: a machine-readable summary of this site lives at /llms.txt.
