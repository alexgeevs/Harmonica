Harmonica

A music player for your own library that isn't shuffle. Its algorithm spends your listening where it still has value — avoiding unwanted repetition at the song, artist and rendition level.

View on GitHub Downloads

The problem with playing your favourites

You love a handful of songs, so you play them — until the novelty wears off and they stop giving you anything back. Ordinary shuffle makes it worse: it keeps serving the same tracks, and the ones you like most get burned out first.

Harmonica treats your attention as the scarce resource it is. Rather than picking at random, it estimates where a listen still has utility and spends it there: cooling down songs you've heard recently, easing off a track you're starting to tire of, and quietly resurfacing things you've forgotten you own. The result is a library that stays fresh instead of collapsing onto the same dozen songs.

What it does

Not shuffle

A utility-maximising queue with cooldowns at the song, group (artist or topic) and sub-group (cover or rendition) levels — so you don't hear the same thing, or the same idea, twice in quick succession.

Satiation guard

Detects when you're wearing out a track and eases it out of rotation before it loses its shine, then lets it recover.

Rediscovery & cold start

Brings dormant songs back into view and makes sure unheard tracks get their turn, so nothing in your library sits untouched forever.

Ratings with mood normalisation

Star your tracks and Harmonica accounts for the mood you rated them in, so a good day doesn't skew the whole library.

Cover & rendition families

Groups alternate versions of a song together so the algorithm can vary the rendition without repeating the piece.

Profiles, stats & real settings

Per-user profiles for a shared household NAS, listening statistics, and settings that are actual switches and sliders — not a config file to hand-edit.

Setting it up

Harmonica is self-hosted, so you run it yourself. Doing that by hand is tedious, so the easiest path is to point an AI coding agent at the repository and let it handle the build and configuration for you.
